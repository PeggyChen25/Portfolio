Brooks Running Forecasting Demand 2027

Objective

The goal of this project was to help Brooks Running forecast product demand for its Spring and Fall 2027 apparel collections. We aimed to identify which product and color attributes drive sales, helping the merchandising team make smarter, data-driven decisions that align with consumer preferences and business goals.

Problem

Brooks faced the challenge of predicting demand across hundreds of styles and colors, balancing new products and carryover items, and planning regionally appropriate assortments. With shifting customer trends and a constantly evolving market, they needed insights to reduce the risk of overstocking or understocking key products.

Action

  • Data preparation: Combined five years of global product and sales data (2020‚Äì2024), engineered features like color family, brightness, and lifecycle stage, and cleaned data using SQL and Python.
  • Exploratory analysis (EDA): Conducted year-over-year (YOY) sales analysis, built time series plots and heatmaps in Tableau, and identified trends in product performance and seasonality.
  • Modeling and forecasting: Ran multivariate regression and SARIMA forecasting models, validated performance with error metrics, and fine-tuned parameters.
  • Visualization and delivery: Built an interactive Tableau dashboard with filters for region, gender, and color, and delivered clear visual and written recommendations for assortment planning.

Result

We identified top-performing color families, recommended an optimal product mix, provided demand forecasts to guide inventory planning, and delivered a self-service dashboard that Brooks can use for future seasonal planning.
