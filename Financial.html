Financial Transaction Analysis

Objective

The goal of this project was to analyze over 2 million financial transactions to segment customers, detect fraud patterns, and predict churn risk. By combining exploratory analysis, clustering, and predictive modeling, we aimed to provide actionable insights that improve customer engagement and reduce risk exposure.

Problem

Financial institutions face massive transaction volumes with complex patterns, making it difficult to identify high-value customers, detect at-risk accounts, and flag fraudulent behavior. Traditional rule-based approaches often miss subtle behavioral signals or generate excessive false alarms, leaving companies vulnerable to revenue loss and compliance risks.

Action

  • Data cleaning & feature engineering: Addressed missing values, removed duplicates, replaced invalid credit limits, and engineered key features such as total_spent, transaction_count, spending variability, and credit utilization to improve modeling.
  • Exploratory analysis (EDA): Performed YOY analysis, log-transformed skewed spending data, and used histograms, boxplots, and scatter plots to understand customer patterns and detect outliers.
  • Customer segmentation: Applied K-Means clustering and PCA to group customers by spending, transaction frequency, and credit usage, identifying high-value segments, low-spenders, and potential risk groups.
  • Fraud detection: Analyzed top fraudulent clients and merchants, used pairplots to explore feature relationships, and built recall-prioritized models to catch 62% of fraud cases despite tradeoffs in precision.
  • Churn prediction: Built predictive models using total spent, transaction count, and spending variability; optimized thresholds to balance precision and recall; evaluated model performance with AUC and feature importance analysis.
  • Visualization: Created dashboards showing risk density, cluster insights, and fraud hotspots, making results clear and accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Result

The analysis provided a clear roadmap for reducing risk and improving customer retention. It uncovered top high-value and at-risk customers, flagged key fraud clusters, and offered actionable recommendations, such as adjusting credit limits, targeting win-back campaigns, and refining fraud monitoring. Predictive models achieved over 95% accuracy for churn and captured 62% of fraud cases, delivering measurable impact to risk management efforts.

Why this matters

This project highlighted the power of combining statistical analysis, clustering, and machine learning to drive business outcomes. By transforming raw financial data into clear, actionable insights, we empowered the organization to better understand its customers, reduce risk exposure, and improve long-term profitability.
